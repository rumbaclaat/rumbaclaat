The story behind Original Reserve

How twelve years of patience in American oak shapes the rum that defines our character.

James Whitfield · 1 December 2025 · 6 min read

The first thing you notice walking into the warehouse isn't the rum. It's the silence. Hundreds of barrels, stacked four high, breathing in and out with the Jamaican climate for years at a time. No one rushes them. No one talks loudly. The work is patience, dressed up as a job.

Twelve years, by design

Original Reserve isn't twelve years old because it sounded like a good number. It's twelve years old because that's when the rum tells us it's ready. Before that, the cask is still asking questions. After that, on a different bottle, the answer might be different. With this one — twelve.

The barrels are ex-bourbon American oak. They arrive emptied of their previous life and ready for a second act. American oak is tighter-grained than European, which means a slower, more deliberate exchange between the wood and the spirit. The wood gives us vanilla, a quiet toasted caramel, a hint of coconut. The spirit gives back its sharpness. The trade is fair.

"You can teach someone to distil in a week. You can't teach them to wait twelve years. That part is character, not craft."
— Master Distiller, Original Reserve

What you taste

The nose opens with vanilla and dried fruit — dark cherry mostly, a little fig — with oak sitting underneath like a steady bass note. The palate is rich without being heavy: caramel, ripe banana, a flash of dark chocolate towards the back. The finish is long, warming, with a spice that builds rather than burns.

Serve neat first. Then with one large ice cube. Don't waste a great rum on a complicated cocktail until you've understood what it tastes like alone.

The reason it works

Premium isn't a price tag. It's a series of decisions made when no one was watching. Choosing barrels because they're the right barrels, not the cheap ones. Resting them where they want to rest, not where it's convenient. Bottling at 43% because that's what holds the character, not 40% because regulation lets you. Each of those decisions, in isolation, is small. Together they're the rum.

That's the only thing we set out to do with Original Reserve: get every small decision right, then leave it alone for twelve years.
