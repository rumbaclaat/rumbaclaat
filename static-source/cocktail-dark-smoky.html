CRAFT COCKTAIL · AFTER DARK

Dark & Smoky

Twelve-year rum, a touch of mezcal smoke, and dark honey. A late-night cocktail for serious drinkers and quiet conversation.

Medium ⏱ 8 mins Evening ★ 4.7 (54)

Served in a rocks glass with 1 large cube.

Ingredients

  • 50ml Rumbaclaat 12 Year Original Reserve
  • 10ml Mezcal (something modest — Espadín)
  • 20ml Dark Honey Syrup (2:1 honey to hot water, cooled)
  • 2 dashes Mole Bitters
  • 1 Wide Orange Peel
GlasswareRocks glass with 1 large cube
GarnishExpressed orange peel
Best withDark chocolate, aged cheese
Total time8 mins

Method

  1. 1

    Combine the rum, mezcal, honey syrup, and bitters in a mixing glass.

  2. 2

    Add ice and stir gently for 30 seconds until well-chilled and properly diluted (taste it — it should not be sharp).

  3. 3

    Strain into a rocks glass over one large single ice cube.

  4. 4

    Express the orange peel over the surface to release its oils, wipe the rim, and drop it in.

BARTENDER'S TIP

Use a bar spoon with a long spiral stem. Stir, don't shake — shaking aerates and would lose the syrupy mouthfeel that makes this drink. The mezcal is there to whisper, not to shout. If you can taste 'mezcal' clearly, you used too much.
